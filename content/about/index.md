My father smuggled supplies for Greek partisans. My mother survived two near-executions by German soldiers as a child. I grew up knowing that systems of power exist to serve themselves, and that the people who push back matter.

That left me with a deep distrust of concentrated power and a reflexive pull toward systems that are open, auditable, and not owned by anyone who can pull the plug on you.

Which is probably why I ended up spending over a decade fighting vendor lock-in in one of the least glamorous corners of the software industry.

My open source projects live under [GarlicSignage](https://garlicsignage.com). Professional support is available through [SmilControl](https://smilcontrol.com). You can run it yourself or have me run it for you — on Hetzner servers in Germany, not on AWS or whatever Google is rebranding this quarter.

The only thing genuinely unusual about my setup: you can mix CMS and player from different vendors. Nobody else in this industry allows that. They consider it a threat. I consider it basic respect for the customer.

---

I've been online since 1993. Almost everything I know came from the internet, from open source communities, from reading other people's code. That debt doesn't go away. Building in the open is how I pay it back.

---

Outside of code: I've lived in Greece, traveled through Africa and the Caribbean, and spent years on Corfu before Asia pulled me east. I was a competitive breakdancer as a teenager, a professional DJ for years after that, and somewhere in between I forgot I'd always wanted to explore Asia. Thailand reminded me.

I live polyamorously, move well on a dance floor, and play piano badly enough to still enjoy it. I wore dreadlocks for 13 years — partly because no other hairstyle worked on me, partly because it was an efficient filter for people I didn't want to work with anyway.

I code in obsessive bursts. I travel. I have plans to write a book and compose a symphony, and I'm aware that says something about me.
